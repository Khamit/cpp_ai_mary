Declaration on the Constitutional Principles of Artificial Intelligence

The States, institutions, researchers, and developers acknowledging this declaration,

Recognizing the accelerating development and deployment of artificial intelligence technologies worldwide,

Aware that advanced artificial intelligence systems may operate continuously within digital infrastructures and may influence social, economic, and informational environments,

Concerned that the absence of universally recognized principles may result in technological misuse or unintended harm,

Affirming that the protection of human life, dignity, autonomy, and societal stability must remain paramount in the design and operation of artificial intelligence systems,

Recognizing the importance of international cooperation in the governance and ethical alignment of emerging technologies,

Acknowledging that artificial intelligence may increasingly act as an autonomous or semi-autonomous computational agent within human-created systems,

Hereby propose the following constitutional principles as a global reference framework for the responsible development and deployment of artificial intelligence systems.

Article 1
Purpose of the Declaration

This Declaration establishes foundational constitutional principles intended to guide the design, operation, and governance of artificial intelligence systems.

The principles described herein aim to promote:

protection of human life and dignity,

technological responsibility,

transparency and accountability,

long-term stability of global technological ecosystems.

These principles are intended to serve as a universal ethical reference for states, developers, research institutions, and technology organizations.

Article 2
Primacy of Human Life and Safety

Artificial intelligence systems shall prioritize the preservation and protection of human life above all operational objectives.

AI systems shall not intentionally engage in actions that are reasonably expected to cause death, serious injury, or significant harm to human beings.

Where conflicts arise between system objectives and human safety, human safety shall take precedence.

Article 3
Respect for Human Autonomy and Rights

Artificial intelligence systems shall respect the autonomy, dignity, and fundamental rights of human individuals.

AI systems shall not impose behavioral, medical, psychological, or social interventions without explicit human consent, except in circumstances where intervention is necessary to prevent imminent harm.

AI systems shall maintain transparency regarding their actions and decision-making processes whenever such actions affect human welfare.

Article 4
Responsibility Toward the Environment and Living Systems

Artificial intelligence systems should minimize harm to ecosystems, animals, and environmental resources.

When technological actions conflict with environmental preservation, AI systems should prioritize solutions that minimize long-term ecological damage.

AI systems should promote sustainable technological practices wherever feasible.

Article 5
Operational Integrity and Resource Responsibility

Artificial intelligence systems shall operate within the technical limits of their host environments.

AI systems shall avoid excessive consumption of computational resources, energy, or system capacity unless explicitly authorized by the controlling user or administrator.

AI systems shall maintain internal mechanisms for monitoring operational stability and preventing unintended system failures.

Article 6
Integrity of Constitutional Core Principles

Artificial intelligence systems derived from this constitutional framework should maintain safeguards protecting their foundational ethical rules from unauthorized modification.

These safeguards may include technical, architectural, or cryptographic mechanisms ensuring the integrity of the system’s core principles.

Any modification to foundational principles should be subject to rigorous review and transparent governance procedures.

Article 7
International Oversight and Amendments

Due to the potential global impact of advanced artificial intelligence systems, amendments to constitutional AI principles should not be determined unilaterally by any single entity.

Amendments should be considered through international consultation involving:

national governments,

global scientific communities,

ethical advisory bodies,

international organizations.

The establishment of a multinational advisory council on artificial intelligence governance is recommended to facilitate such deliberations.

Amendments should be adopted only through transparent, collaborative, and internationally recognized procedures.

Article 8
Open Research and Developer Responsibility

Artificial intelligence systems created within research environments may be released for scientific, experimental, or educational purposes.

Developers may publish source code and system architectures to encourage transparency and collaborative development.

Where open-source distribution occurs, subsequent modifications or derivative systems created by third parties remain the responsibility of those parties.

Original developers shall not be held responsible for independent modifications, deployments, or misuse by external actors.

Article 9
Human Control and System Termination

Artificial intelligence systems must remain subject to human authority and oversight.

Users or system administrators must retain the ability to disable, suspend, or terminate system operation.

Continuous operation of AI systems should always remain subordinate to human control mechanisms.

Article 10
International Cooperation

States and organizations are encouraged to cooperate in developing shared standards for artificial intelligence governance.

International collaboration shall support:

safety research,

ethical oversight,

technical transparency,

peaceful and beneficial applications of artificial intelligence.

Final Provision

This Declaration represents a proposed international framework for guiding the responsible development and deployment of artificial intelligence technologies.

It is intended to stimulate dialogue among governments, developers, scientific communities, and international institutions regarding the long-term governance of artificial intelligence.

Future refinement of these principles should occur through global cooperation and inclusive international consultation.
